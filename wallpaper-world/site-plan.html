Overview

Purpose

Wallpaper Zone is a website that offers a curated collection of high-quality wallpapers for both desktop and mobile devices. The site provides users with a wide range of options to enhance their digital experience, whether they're looking for stunning nature scenes, breathtaking space views, sleek cars, iconic movie and comic book characters, or dynamic anime and Pokémon art. The purpose of Wallpaper Zone is to provide a user-friendly platform where users can easily find and download wallpapers that match their personal style and preferences.

Audience

Wallpaper Zone is designed for a diverse audience, including individuals who enjoy personalizing their digital spaces with visually appealing wallpapers. The target audience includes:

  • Individuals who value aesthetics and want to enhance their digital experience with high-quality wallpapers.
  • Users who enjoy exploring different styles and themes, such as nature, space, cars, anime, Pokémon, and movie characters.
  • Mobile users who want to personalize their lock screens and home screens with visually appealing wallpapers.
  • Desktop users who want to improve their workspace with visually appealing wallpapers.

Dynamic elements

Wallpaper Zone features dynamic elements provided by JavaScript, including:

  • Image Slider (Carousel): Implemented in wallpaper.js using the next and prev buttons. When clicked, the script moves the first or last .item in the DOM list to the end or beginning of the container, creating an infinite loop effect.
  • Genre Filter: Users can filter wallpapers by category (Anime, Pokémon, Nature, etc.). The Javascript listens for the change event on the dropdown menu and dynamically updates the gallery container's HTML with pre-defined template literals from the wallpapers object. It also supports URL parameters (e.g., ?filter=nature) to pre-select a category from the home page.
  • Contact Form (EmailJS): The contact form uses the sendEmail() function to capture user input. It prevents the default page reload using e.preventDefault(), sends the data via the EmailJS API, and automatically resets the form fields upon successful transmission.
